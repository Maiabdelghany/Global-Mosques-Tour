MOSQUE OF AMR EBN ELAS

The Mosque of Amr ibn al-As is one of the most prominent mosques in Egypt, located in the Old Cairo neighborhood. It is considered the first mosque built in Egypt.

  • Foundation:The construction of the mosque began in the year 21 AH (642 CE) by the Islamic leader Amr ibn al-As after the conquest of Egypt.
  • Opening:It was completed in the same year and represents the first mosque built in Egypt.

    • Architecture

  • Design: The mosque features traditional Islamic architecture, with a spacious courtyard, a central prayer area and beautiful columns.
  • Minaret: It has a magnificent minaret, which is one of its most notable features.

    • The uniqe features of Amr ibn al-As Mosque

  • simple and Distinctive Design: The mosque is characterized by its simple design, with a large open space not divided by rooms or complex columns. It consists of a central courtyard surrounded by arcades, reflecting the traditional Islamic style focused on functionality and spirituality.
  • Multiple Expansions and Renovations: The mosque has undergone several expansions and renovations across different eras, from the Umayyad to the Fatimid, Mamluk, and Ottoman periods. Each era left its architectural mark, giving the mosque a unique diversity in design.
  • Distinctive Minaret: The mosque features a minaret with a unique design, added during the Umayyad period. It is one of the oldest minarets in Egypt and adds a special charm to the mosque.

  • Strategic Location: Located in Fustat, Egypt's first Islamic capital, the mosque occupies a strategic location. This made it a primary destination for Muslims and travelers interested in visiting this historical landmark.

Importance

  • The mosque hosts numerous religious and social activities, including lessons and sermons.

    • Best Time to Visit the Mosque of Amr Ibn Al-As in Egypt

  • The best time to visit the Mosque of Amr Ibn Al-As in Egypt is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November). During these times, the temperatures are moderate, making the visit more comfortable.

    Tips for Visiting

  • Early morning or evening:These times are less crowded and more peaceful
  • Avoid summer: In the summer months, temperatures can be very high, making the visit uncomfortable.
  • Holidays and occasions:You can visit the mosque during Islamic holidays, where there is a beautiful festive atmosphere, but it may be more crowded.
