Sheikh Zayed Mosque

The well-known Sheikh Zayed Mosque is actually located in Abu Dhabi, the capital of the United Arab Emirates. Named after Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, The founder and first president of The United Arab Emirates. stands as one of the most prominent religious and tourist landmarks in the UAE and it is also considred as the sixth largest mosque in the world.

    • Foundation: The Emirati ruler Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan aimed to build this mosque to establish and deepen Islamic culture.
    • Opening:It was inaugurated in 2007 after construction began in 1996.

      • Architecture

    • Design:
      It includes four minarets and 82 white marble domes. It was built using the finest quality raw materials. Its area covering 412,22 square meters.
    • Historical:
      • The first prayer held there was the Eid al-Adha prayer in the year 1428 AH (December 19, 2007).
    • The mosque can accommodate more than 7,000 worshippers inside, but the outdoor areas can be utilized to accommodate up to 52,000 worshippers.

      • Best Time to Visit

    • The best time to visit the Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque in Abu Dhabi is typically during the cooler months, from November to March. During this time, temperatures are mild, ranging from 18°C to 30°C (64°F to 86°F), making it comfortable to explore the mosque and its beautiful outdoor spaces.>

      • Recommended Times

    • Early Morning or Late Afternoon:provides a quieter experience with fewer crowds.
    • Sunset:Late afternoon visits allow you to experience both daylight and sunset views, as the mosque lights up beautifully in the evening.
    • Evening:The mosque’s unique lighting system, designed to reflect the lunar phases, makes evening visits especially stunning.
