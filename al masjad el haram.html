Al-Masjid al-Haram

The Great Mosque of Mecca is the biggest mosque in the world.
It is located in Mecca, Saudi Arabia.

History

  • Area: The mosque covers over 356,800 square meters.
  • Origins: The mosque began in the 7th century and has undergone many expansions.
  • Current Structure: It has grown significantly to accommodate millions of visitors.

Practical Tips

  • Dress Code: Wear comfy clothes. Men wear ihram, while women should wear loose clothes and cover their hair.
  • Stay Hydrated: Mecca can be hot, so drink lots of water.
  • Footwear: Wear comfy shoes since you will be walking a lot.

Visitor Information

  • Accessibility: The mosque is designed to accommodate millions of visitors. Facilities include ramps and elevators for ease of access.
  • Safety and Security: There are extensive security measures in place to ensure the safety of all visitors.

Pilgrimage

  • Hajj: One of the Five Pillars of Islam; every able Muslim is required to perform Hajj at least once in their lifetime.
  • Umrah: A lesser pilgrimage that can be performed at any time of the year, it includes rituals similar to those of Hajj but is not obligatory.

Landmarks

Black Stone

It is an oval-shaped stone in the eastern corner of the Kaaba. It was white but turned black. Muslims point to it during Tawaf.

Maqam Ibrahim

This is a stone with the footprints of Prophet Ibrahim, located near the Kaaba. It is a place for prayer after Tawaf during Hajj and Umrah.

Well of Zamzam

This ancient well produces blessed water. It is a miracle by God for Hagar and her son Ismael, and its water is considered sacred by Muslims.
