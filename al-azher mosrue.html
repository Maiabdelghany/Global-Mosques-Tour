Al-Azhar Mosque

Al-Azhar Mosque is one of the oldest and most famous mosques. It was established in Cairo, Egypt, in the historical area of Hussein, known for its many Islamic and historical landmarks. Founded in 970 AD (359 AH), it is considered an important center for Islamic sciences and religious studies.

Establishment and History:

  • Foundation:Al-Azhar Mosque was established in 970 AD (359 AH) by order of the Fatimid Caliph al-Mu'izz li-Din Allah. It was built as part of the planning for Cairo to be the capital of the Fatimid state.
  • name:The name "Al-Azhar" is believed to be derived from the name of Fatimah al-Zahra, the daughter of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), symbolizing light and beauty.
    • Al-Azhar is also referred to as "Al-Azhar Al-Sharif," symbolizing moderation and balance in Islam. It leads efforts to combat extremism and promote values of tolerance. The mosque has five uniquely designed minarets, added at different times, which are prominent features that add a beautiful touch and symbolize its significance in the Islamic world.

Architecture

  • Design: Al-Azhar is known for its unique architectural style that reflects Islamic architectural influences across different eras, from the Fatimid to Mamluk and Ottoman styles.
